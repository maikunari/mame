## Design Context

### Users
A single user: Mike, a builder who ships AI-augmented developer tools and runs his own infra in Kamakura, Japan. Reads the digest at his desk in the morning over coffee — this is *his* publication, not a feed for an audience.

The "alumni circle — this week in A.I." printed-magazine reference and now Anthropic.com/news's clean editorial layout are the two anchor references. Mike is sophisticated, opinionated, hates "AI slop" aesthetics, and pushes back hard on unnecessary complexity.

### Use Cases
- **Morning read**: 5-15 min daily review of bookmarks that would otherwise rot in a drawer
- **Skimming with depth-on-demand**: glance the signal headline + section titles → pull on whichever item rewards attention
- **Context bridging**: Old Gold callbacks reconnect today's drift to older saved items he'd forgotten

This is a publication, not a dashboard. There are no widgets, no metrics, no "engagement." The success metric is whether Mike actually opens it tomorrow.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **weathered, deliberate, idiosyncratic.**

- **Weathered**: this isn't slick startup minimalism. It should feel like a coat that fits well — paper-warm, a little serious, the work of someone who reads.
- **Deliberate**: every item was saved by Mike on purpose; the magazine treats them with the same care, never with feed-scroll entropy.
- **Idiosyncratic**: it's HIS magazine. The masthead says "Kamakura, Japan." The "AI" in "dAIly digest" is a visual accent. Local touches matter.

Emotional goal: the moment of opening should feel like picking up a thin literary quarterly that arrived in the mail, not like checking notifications.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Editorial print-magazine register**, NOT consumer SaaS dashboard. Influences: The New Yorker contents page, The Believer covers, NYRB, Ben Pieratt's old work, fine periodical typography. Layered onto Anthropic.com/news's clean structural foundation: generous whitespace, asymmetric layout, restrained accent color, high typographic hierarchy.

**Theme**: light. Warm cream paper, near-black ink. This is read in the morning. Reduce-friction, daylight reading.

**Anti-references**: BuzzFeed; Medium-style "claps and authorless cards"; any AI-news aggregator with purple gradients; admin-dashboard density; web2 "magazine themes" with hero-image clutter.

### Design Principles
1. **Treat each item as content, not a card.** No drop-shadow rectangles. Type, leading, and white space do the structural work.
2. **One-column reading width.** ~65ch body. This is a reading interface, not a layout exercise.
3. **High-contrast type, restrained color.** Solid ink on warm cream. A single warm accent (yellow highlight for keywords, deep red for the masthead "AI") earns its rarity.
4. **Numbered sections as editorial gesture, not navigation.** The "01" / "02" markers are typographic, large, set in display serif — they're decoration that orients the reader.
5. **Why-it-matters as a sidebar voice.** Each item carries its summary in body register and its why-it-matters as a quieter aside (smaller, italic, indented or highlighted) — the editor's whisper to the reader.
